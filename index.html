Theme Selection

We choose the theme collectively as {m}aganda staff in the fall semester. The theme attempts to address a political, cultural or societal issue that we believe is important to raise awareness for in that particular year. Once our theme is chosen, the Editor-in-Chief and Literary Editor work closely to create a Call for Submissions, usually a poem or prose piece, and/or illustration, to capture what the theme means to the staff and provoke thoughts around the theme for potential contributors.

{m}30 theme will be announced in early October!

Submission Process

We release this call for submissions in the fall semester of the school year; close submissions at the end of January in the spring semester; and publish by the end of the school year. All work must be submitted in accordance with our submissions guidelines. We accept submissions from anyone and everyone. You do not have to be Filipino to submit.

For instructions on how to submit see How to Submit in the "publication" tab

For more info on what to submit see Call For Submissions in the "publication" tab

Designing the {m}agazine

The publication is edited and designed by the {m}aganda staff and interns, all of whom are UC Berkeley students. During the Spring semester, {m}aganda's layout editor or layout consultant creates and releases a style guide containing the fonts, color scheme, and over-all design tips for the issue. We then utilize Adobe InDesign to create all of the pages of the magazine. Each intern and staff member is given the freedom to design multiple pages of the magazine given that they follow the style guide.

Getting {m} published

The magazine is published in late April, and released by way of a Launch Reception + Open Mic in early May before the end of the school year.

SUBMIT TO {m}30

WHO CAN SUBMIT

You do not have to be Pilipino in order to submit. We appreciate creative works from any and everyone; we hope you can encourage the greater community around you to submit as well. In addition, we are not limited solely to prose and poetry, but also any type of art you can produce. You may choose to submit anonymously as well.

WHAT TO SUBMIT

Calling for submissions of any media, from prose to poetry to photography to artwork and music.

  • literary submit by mail or as a file attachment by email
  • visual prints/slides by mail or email attachment (greater than 300 dpi)
  • audio cd by mail or mp3 by email
  • film/animation avi format (10 min max)
WHERE TO SUBMIT

Send an EMAIL per submission to maganda.literary@gmail.com

FORMAT SUBJECT LINE AS FOLLOWS:

last name, first name, name of piece, medium

ex:Vallida, Raymar, Salat, short story

JOIN OUR INTERNSHIP PROGRAM

The purpose of this internship journey is to give students hands-on experience of the unique ability of art in empowering individuals through the expression of self, as well as the collective spirit of their community and political history.

Students will work closely as interns, learning how to work within a literary arts organization in a variety of aspects -- ranging from public relations, marketing, to the production of the magazine -- while acquiring specialized skills that are essential for the functioning of each component, such as graphic design for layouts, CD programs for working with media, etc.

By playing a supporting role and learning the responsibilities of each component of the organization, students will participate in {m}aganda’s mission to provide the community with spaces in which they can express themselves through various mediums of art.

In addition, students will learn about {m}aganda magazine’s roots as a vehicle through which Berkeley’s Pilipinx and Pilipinx-American community share their views and issues. Interns will also learn about our community's expanding role in engaging people in critical dialogue about the issues of marginalized communities. {m}aganda serves to educate, collaborate, support, express, and empower our community and beyond.
